I am writing to inform you of a security incident that was detected on our Exchange servers last night at approximately 11:47 PM EST. Our monitoring systems flagged unusual outbound traffic from the mail relay cluster (EX-NODE-04 and EX-NODE-07).

Preliminary analysis indicates that an unauthorized actor gained access to several mailboxes in the Finance department through a compromised service account. The affected accounts include:

- jmartin@purviewexchange.com (Jennifer Martin, CFO)
- rthompson@purviewexchange.com (Robert Thompson, Controller)
- mchen@purviewexchange.com (Michelle Chen, Accounts Payable)

The attacker appears to have used a credential stuffing technique, exploiting a service account (svc-backup-mail) that had not been rotated since Q3 2023 in violation of our password rotation policy.

Immediate actions taken:
1. Disabled the compromised service account
2. Forced password reset on all affected mailboxes
3. Enabled enhanced logging on all Exchange nodes
4. Notified our incident response vendor (CrowdStrike)

We need to conduct a full forensic review of the mail flow logs for the past 30 days. I've also flagged this to Legal as we may have GDPR and SOX notification obligations.
